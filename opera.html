THE SORCERER

ACT 2 Exterior of Sir Marmaduke's mansion by moonlight. All the peasantry are discovered asleep on the ground, as at the end of Act I.

MEN. Why, where be oi, and what be oi a doin', A sleepin' out, just when the dews du rise?

GIR. Why, that's the very way your health to ruin, And don't seem quite respectable likewise!

MEN.

(staring at girls)

Eh, that's you! Only think o' that now!

GIR.

(coyly)

What may you be at, now? Tell me, du! (admiringly) Eh, what a nose, And eh, what eyes, miss! Lips like a rose, And cheeks likewise, miss!

GIR.

(coyly)

Oi tell you true, Which I've never done, sir, Oi loike you As I never loiked none, sir! ALL. Eh, but oi du loike you! If you'll marry me, I'll dig for you and rake for you!

GIR. If you'll marry be, I'll scrub for you and bake for you!

MEN. If you'll marry me, all others I'll forsake for you! ALL. All this will I du, if you marry me!

GIR. If you'll marry me, I'll cook for you and brew for you!

MEN. If you'll marry me, I've guineas not a few for you!

GIR. If you'll marry me, I'll take you in and du for you! ALL. All this will I du, if you'll marry me! Eh, but I do loike you!
